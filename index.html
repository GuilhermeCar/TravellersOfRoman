Travellers of Roman
Peregrini Romani

Synopsis

English

Three teenagers, Lukas, Gabriel and Agatha, decide to hide in the school library after closing time to study for an important test. When the librarian discovers them, they run desperately out the exit door. However, when crossing it, something inexplicable happens: the environment around them transforms, and they find themselves in a completely different world — the streets are made of stones, the air has a different smell, and the people speak a strange language. They are in Ancient Rome. Disoriented, the three friends soon realize that they need to learn to turn around quickly. Without understanding Latin, they are unable to communicate, leaving them at the mercy of the locals. After days of wandering the streets and closely observing the habits of the Romans, Lucas, Marina and Gabriel manage to learn the basics of the language and begin to integrate minimally. However, this adaptation comes with setbacks. In an incident at the market, Gabriel almost gets into a fight with a merchant, and it is Marina who saves the trio by teaming up with Livia, an influential woman who starts to protect them. Lucas embarks on a search to understand how they ended up there and how they could return to the present. He begins visiting temples and forums, investigating Roman myths and religions. Gabriel, increasingly fascinated by the world of gladiators, sees in Rome an opportunity to achieve the glory he always dreamed of. Meanwhile, Marina engages with the women of high society, learning how women's subtle influences shape politics and power. As the three adapt to Roman life, their paths begin to diverge. Lucas discovers an ancient quote about Hercules, which makes him wonder if his trip to Rome would be a test from the gods. He becomes obsessed with finding the portal that will bring them back to the present. Marina, in turn, establishes a network of contacts among the city's influential women, learning the complex political intrigues of Rome. Gabriel, enchanted by the life of gladiators, decides that the glory of the arenas is the destiny he always wanted. However, time travel was not an accident. Lucas discovers an ancient ritual that can open the portal, but which requires a definitive choice from each person: stay in the past or return to the future. On the night of the ritual, Gabriel decides to remain in Rome, seduced by the idea of ​​becoming a legend. Lucas and Marina, despite the adventures and friendships they made, choose to return to the present, knowing that their lives in the future still hold many opportunities. Back in the library, no time seems to have passed, but they carry the memories and scars of their journey in Rome. While Gabriel becomes part of history as a hero lost in time, Lucas and Marina return to modern life, transformed by the experience, but with the weight of a definitive farewell.
